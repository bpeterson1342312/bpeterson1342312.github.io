The Foggy Mountain Boys with Flats and Scruggs was an American bluegrass band that was active from 1948-1969. Lester Flats and Earl Scruggs were the founding members. The other band members changed through the years. Paul Warren, Josh Graves, Jake Tullock, and Curly Seckler were notable musicians that were once members of the band. The Foggy Mountain Boys were also seen as one of the landmark bands in bluegrass music.

The Foggy Mountain Boys were the featured band on The Lester Flat and Earl Scruggs Grand Ole Opry Show. The group released many albums and singles during the twenty-one years they played together. Four of their thirteen albums reached the top ten on the country charts. "The Ballard of Jed Clampett" peaked at number one on the charts. This is the theme song for The "Beverly Hillbillies."

"Foggy Mountain Breakdown" was an instrumental released in 1949. It won two Grammy Awards. It can be heard in many movie car chase scenes. The band also played the theme song for the TV show "Petticoat Junction" and the jingle for Martha White flour. The Martha White flour jingle is still used today.
